Pillar 2 — Roles

How knowledge is transferred through leadership and mentoring

Core idea from the whiteboard
Members in leadership positions "mentor" less experienced members — via tasks. Whoever holds a leadership role is automatically also a knowledge transmitter. This is not an extra task — it is the main task of leads.
Role structure Three levels — clearly separated
Member — active in a department, learning, executing tasks
Lead / Head — owns a department or project, mentors members
Board — overall strategic responsibility
Progression through: experience + knowledge transfer contribution — not just time served
Expert interviews Securing knowledge before it leaves
When: 4 to 6 weeks before end of term
Format: 45-min conversation with successor, recorded or minuted
Output: 1-page summary into the department repository
How-to templates For recurring tasks
Organizing a hackathon — from partner outreach to closing ceremony
Inviting a speaker — email template, briefing, logistics
Running a sponsoring conversation — preparation, pitch, follow-up
Onboarding a new member — step by step
Mentoring via tasks Knowledge through work
Pair-tasking: every important task done at least once by experienced + new member together
Shadowing: new members join leads at sponsor meetings, speaker calls
Handover tasks: departing leads create the tasks for their successors themselves

Pillar 3 — Alumni & Network

Alumni are not ex-members — they are the network

Alumni directory Make the network visible
Each alumni entry: name, current position, expertise, mentoring availability
Searchable by: company, field, country, cohort year
Maintenance: annual update email to all alumni
Tool: Notion database or Airtable — simple, low overhead
Mentorship program Structured 1:1 mentoring
Matching: member states interest area → matched with relevant alumni
Format: 3 months, 1 call per month, clear agenda
Kick-off: once per year — "Mentoring Match Event"
What alumni get: PushQuantum stays relevant in their lives, visibility, appreciation
Active role for alumni
Guest speaker: alumni give 1 talk per year at PushQuantum events
Challenge giver: alumni companies provide hackathon challenges
Door opener: alumni connect current members to companies for sponsoring and internships
"PQ Marriage" — the goal: alumni feel lifelong belonging to the community
Continuity through alumni
Strategic advisor role: 2 to 3 alumni on the advisory board — continuity across board changes
Institutional memory calls: new board meets old board once per year for lessons learned
Emergency contact list: in a crisis — who has solved this problem before?

Member Journey

From first application to lifelong alumni advisor

Step 1
New member

Step 2
Dept. member
Pair-tasking with experienced member, first own tasks
Step 3
Experienced member
Mentors new members, contributes to how-to docs
Step 4
Lead / Head
Owns department, expert interview before end of term
Step 5
Alumni
Directory entry, mentoring offer, active role
Step 6
Alumni advisor
Strategic advisor, door opener, emergency contact
Transition protocols — what happens at each step
Member to Lead: shadowing period (4 weeks) + handover conversation + update dept. doc
Lead to Alumni: expert interview (45 min) + summary into repository + directory entry
Board election: new board meets old board — 2h institutional knowledge session
Annual rhythm: review of all how-to docs once per year — are they still current?
